Overview
What's happening to Orbit?

Orbit has been acquired by Postman.
‍
Over the next 90 days, the existing Orbit product and platform will be winding down. We won’t allow new users or new workspaces to be created. But as a current users, you will be able to add users to your existing workspace, as allowed by your plan type.

On May 31, we will stop ingesting new data from connected data sources. However, you will still be able to access and explore your workspace, as well as export your community data.

On July 11, we will disable all functionality.

What is going to happen to my Orbit Account?

Your account will continue to function until the last day, July 11. Anytime before this date, we encourage you to export your community data to retain complete access to your information.

How can I Export my data?

We have provided steps within the app to support the process of migrating or exporting your data. Here are detailed guides to get you started:

  • Export data as a CSV

  • Export data through the Orbit API
Billing and subscription
What will happen if I’m on an annual plan?

If you have an annual subscription, we will contact you directly to process a refund for the prorated amount remaining on your current term.

What will happen to my monthly plan?

‍If you’re on a monthly plan, we will stop charging you at the end of May, and until then, you can cancel any time.

Can I start a paid subscription during this time?

To ensure a smooth transition for our current users, we will not be accepting new subscriptions during the 90-day period.

Data Base
What happens to my data after the 90 days?

After the 90-day period, all user data stored on Orbit will be permanently deleted. We recommend exporting or migrating your data before this deadline to keep your data.

Will Postman have access to my data?

No, Postman (or any other entity) will not have access to your personal data without your explicit consent. Data privacy and protection are our top priorities.

Can I delete my Orbit workspace/account and remove my community and personal data?

Absolutely. Should you choose to delete your workspace or account from Orbit, here are the steps to follow:

  • Export or Migrate your data. We recommend that you export or migrate your data out of Orbit to ensure you retain access to your valuable information.

  • Subsequently, if you're the workspace owner, you can proceed to delete the workspace

Once these steps are completed, you will be able to fully delete your account, effectively removing all your community and personal information from our system.

Features and Functions
Can I sign up for a new account during the transition period?

To ensure a smooth transition for our current users, we will not be accepting new account sign-ups during the 90-day period.

Will there be any changes to the features and functionality of Orbit during the transition?

During the transition period, the Orbit app will remain operational with its current features and functionality intact. However, to facilitate a smooth transition

  • Effective today, we will suspend the addition of new integrations to a workspace and the creation of new workspaces.

  • On May 31, we will stop ingesting new data from connected data sources. However, you will still be able to access and explore your workspace, as well as export your community data.

This approach ensures that we can focus on providing a stable and consistent experience for all users until the transition is complete.

Orbit Community
What will happen to the Orbit Discord ?

Our Orbit discord will remain available although less active. You can always go there for access to resources.
